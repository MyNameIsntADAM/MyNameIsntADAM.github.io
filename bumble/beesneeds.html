California

Bees’ Needs California is our locally focused community outreach program. To educate the youth community on the importance of bees in our environment and their impact on agriculture, Bees’ Needs California plants bee-and-drought friendly gardens at elementary schools. This teaches the youth of the community the responsibility of taking care of a garden and the significance of bees in our surroundings. Here are some projects Bumble has completed so far...

International

Bees’ Needs International, our global outreach program, donates a portion of Bumble hives to impoverished communities in South America, stimulating jobs and improving economic conditions. By introducing Bumble beehives into impoverished areas, not only do we help the local economy, we also contribute to the revival and sustainability of local ecosystems. Take a look at some of the ways Bumble has improved economic and environmental conditions on a global scale...

Bees' Needs - Machokos, Kenya

Bees' Needs International

Bumble had 22 employees with over 100 combined years of honeybee experience...but no one expected elephants. In Kenya, many local villages are susceptible to elephant stampedes, disastrous phenomenons that can level a village in less than a minute. Bumble hives were used not only to produce honey, but also as a natural deterrent to the African elephant, a species deathly afraid of our little pollinating friends.

  • Date: November 2016

Bees' Needs - Recife, Brazil Beehive Donation and Upkeep

Bees' Needs International

At nearly 32 degrees celsius and over 2 million square miles in total area, the Amazon rain forest is perhaps one of nature's most exotic natural landscapes, and one of Bumble's greatest concerns. Working with local engineers and sustenance farmers, Bumble was able to donate, upkeep, and sustain 5 of our own hives for Amazon use. Producing more than 150 pounds a year, these hives provide an additional source of income and sustenance for locals.

  • Date: November 2016
